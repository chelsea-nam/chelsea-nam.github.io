Hi, I'm Chelsea Nam

Tropical Cyclone Researcher

I am a PhD candidate studying convective structure and evolution during tropical cyclone genesis. To better understand the complex, multi-scale tropical cyclogenesis, I use a number of different datasets and methods including radar (airborne, shipborne) data, ensemble high-resolution mesoscale models (WRF and CM1) and large-scale reanalyses.

I started my PhD at Colorado State University (CSU) in 2017 after I received a B.S in earth science from Seoul National University (SNU) at South Korea in 2015, and a M.S in atmospheric science from SNU in 2017.
